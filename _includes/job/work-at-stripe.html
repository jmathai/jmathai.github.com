I Understand User Experience

I believe user experience resides in the sum of every small detail. How can you help users accomplish complex goals as simply as possible while eliminating surprises? Accepting credit card payments seems straightforward at first glance. Simply have the necessary form fields and a submit button. But a great payment experience needs to handle a multitude of challenges.

For example, it's likely that a user will mistype their credit card number. Are there ways to make it easier and less error prone for someone to transcribe 16 numbers from a card to their keyboard? There's many variants of this and solving them elegantly takes deep thinking and understanding of real world user interactions. I appreciate that Stripe has basically set the gold standard here and I'm excited at the opportunity to help.

Part of this article is to convince you that I do indeed think this way. Here is an oldie but goodie. At an earlier startup, we wanted to make it easy for users to browse photo collections consisting of thousands of photos. We felt that infinite scrolling would be an improvement over the traditional paginated experience we were using. However, we did not want to lose the ability to deep link into a photo collection or break the functionality of the browser's back button. This took a lot of thinking and ultimately inventing a new experience we had never seen in the wild.
